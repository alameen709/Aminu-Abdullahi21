Gwandu is famous for so many things amongst which are, scholar articles and ... farming, which is why people called it Gwandu garin makaranta da manoma which translate "Land of readers and farmers". "Gwandu ta Abdullahi", The main reason behind this slogan is because of its legend Islamic schorlar Mallam Abdullahin Gwandu. A person who wrote so many books about Islam and some aspect of life. Among his books are, Tazyin al-waraqat, Diya' al-Sultan Diya' al-Hukkam. Some scholars called him Balaraben kasar hausa (an Arab from Hausa land) due to his dedication and books he wrote in arabic. Some even come to Gwandu just to visits his tomb. This is why in the whole world there is hardly, a single society of scholars that do not know or hear about Gwandu. Gwandu is unique for its tasty and cherished (Kilishi). Anyone who visits Gwandu without tasting their Kilishi has lost out completely. Gwandu is home to farmers. They grow; sugarcane, onion, millet, red and green pepper and also rear cattle and sheep. Gwandu has favorable geographical location and the presence of fertile lands. The area boasted rich pasture lands, which attracted Fulani pastoralists to settle there.
